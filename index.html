About
images/photo.jpg
Raechel Padua
Ontario, Canada

I started learning 3D in 2012, drawn in by the idea that the same tools could build entirely different worlds — movies, games, stylised or realistic, each with its own language. That curiosity hasn't really stopped.

Since then my work has taken me in directions I didn't expect: VR reconstructions of heritage architecture, motion capture for Canada's first fully Deaf musical, procedural materials that became full-scale concrete panels on a real building. Each project taught me to adapt quickly, collaborate across disciplines, and push tools further than their intended use.

Outside of 3D, I compose, produce, and record music — a habit that keeps my creative thinking sharp and occasionally bleeds into how I approach sound and motion in my work.
